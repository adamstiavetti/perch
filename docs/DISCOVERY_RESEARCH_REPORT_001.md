# Discovery Research Report 001

Working name note: "Deadhead Club" is a working product name pending legal and trademark clearance. This report does not claim legal or trademark clearance.

Product principle: Utility first. Community second. Social feed last.

Identity principle: Verified privately. Anonymous publicly. Accountable internally.

Classification: desk research synthesis only. This report records founder/assistant-provided Discovery Research Pass 1 findings against the existing source base in `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`. It does not mark any assumption as user-validated, expert-reviewed, or beta-validated.

FA expert interview status: pending. The founder's wife / FA expert interview remains required before any expert-reviewed or user-validation claims are made.

## 1. Executive Verdict

The discovery evidence supports continuing. The strongest supported wedge is not a generic crew social network. It is a verified aviation-worker utility community:

- Base intel.
- Layover boards.
- Verified and anonymous crew discussion.
- Career and community knowledge.
- AI that summarizes and organizes messy knowledge.

The research supports continuing toward an M1A splash/waitlist asset as a validation tool if explicitly approved. It does not yet justify deeper product features, private app workflows, database implementation, verification uploads, or beta launch.

Real user interviews and the pending FA expert interview are still required.

## 2. Competitor Findings

### Flight Crew View

Desk-research finding:

- Strong at schedule/roster utility, hotel information, crew chat, flight information, airport information, KCM, commuter tools, discounts, and paid subscriptions.
- Public listing signals a large existing crew user base, strong app-store ratings, and subscription pricing.
- Important risk signal: at least one review pattern indicates company/cybersecurity concern around schedule login behavior.

Product implication:

- Deadhead Club should not compete on schedule import, legality, roster workflows, or airline portal integrations in V1.
- Preserve no airline portal login and no schedule scraping.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: Flight Crew View App - App Store.
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: Flight Crew View Support - Delta schedule import.

### StaffTraveler

Desk-research finding:

- Strong at non-rev load requests and standby travel support.
- Claims a large global airline-employee/non-rev community.
- Verification approach includes airline work email and manual proof options.
- Existing research notes indicate verification materials may be deleted after activation.

Product implication:

- Deadhead Club should not attempt flight-load requests in V1.
- StaffTraveler supports tiered verification and delete-after-review handling as a product pattern.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: StaffTraveler - What is StaffTraveler?
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: StaffTraveler - How do I prove that I work(ed) for an airline?

### CrewLounge CONNECT

Desk-research finding:

- Strong at roster/calendar utility, schedule sharing, privacy settings, layover meetups, rest calculation, hotel-room/Wi-Fi memory, pickup/wakeup tools, and things to do.

Product implication:

- Do not lead with roster/calendar integrations.
- Privacy controls and trip utility are useful signals, but they are not Deadhead Club's V1 competition lane.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: CrewLounge CONNECT.
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: CrewLounge CONNECT App Store.

### CrewVIP

Desk-research finding:

- Strong at crew discounts, AI layover planning, nearby crew connection, verified aviation staff membership, location-based offers, and business-owner deal placement.

Product implication:

- AI layover planning and deals are validated as useful surfaces, but not enough to differentiate alone.
- Deadhead Club should be more privacy-first, community-intelligence-first, and less dependent on location or nearby-crew mechanics.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: CrewVIP.

### YoFly Crew

Desk-research finding:

- Appears to target pilots and flight attendants with real-time layover alerts, crew-only chats, crash pad listings, marketplace features, and an anonymous Vent Room.
- Treat this as competitive positioning signal, not proven traction unless independently verified.

Product implication:

- This is a close conceptual competitor.
- Deadhead Club must differentiate through broader airline workforce inclusion, stronger trust/safety, and structured base intelligence.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: YoFly Crew.

### Creweaze

Desk-research finding:

- Layover companion/community for cabin crew with crew-only recommendations, perks, and verification language.
- Public listing signals suggest an early or low-download product.

Product implication:

- Validates the layover recommendation/community niche.
- Does not displace Deadhead Club's broader wedge around base intel, verified anonymous discussion, and wider airline-worker inclusion.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: Creweaze - Google Play.

### Rendezvous

Desk-research finding:

- Layover matching/social for aircrew with trip matching, privacy matching, friend network, messaging, and radar-style concepts.

Product implication:

- Validates the layover connection problem.
- Reinforces privacy risks around schedule matching, location, and nearby crew.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: Rendezvous App - App Store.

### Air Crew Meet

Desk-research finding:

- Aviation social/matching app for pilots, cabin crew, and aviation professionals, with swipe/chat/meetup/career hub.

Product implication:

- Avoid dating/swipe/match-first identity.
- Deadhead Club should remain utility and community knowledge first.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: Air Crew Meet.

## 4. Public Pain Signals

Strongly supported by desk research:

- Non-rev/commuter pain is real, but already strongly owned by StaffTraveler.
- Layover planning and crew recommendations are real and widely represented in competitor products.
- Crew connection, loneliness, and meetups appear repeatedly but are privacy-sensitive.
- Verified professional anonymity is supported by Blind-style model and YoFly Vent Room positioning.

Medium supported by desk research:

- Base-specific institutional knowledge is strategically strong but public evidence is indirect. Existing sources show base assignment, reserve, commuting, and seniority matter, but no clear structured base-intel owner emerged.
- Career mobility and professional tools are plausible and supported by some competitor positioning, but not yet strong enough for V1 core.

Weak / still needs interviews:

- Gate, ramp, dispatch, and scheduler-specific pain signals were weaker in public research.
- Broad inclusion remains strategically valuable, but direct interviews are needed.

## 5. Trust and Safety Findings

Desk research supports strict controls around:

- Airport security procedures.
- Live operational details.
- Passenger private information.
- Sensitive company or security material.
- Exact public crew hotel exposure.
- Live location tracking.
- Employer-visible verification risk.

The current safety boundary should be preserved:

- No portal credentials.
- No schedule scraping.
- No exact public hotel exposure.
- No live location tracking.
- No passenger information.
- No security procedures.
- No badge uploads on public waitlist.
- No AI verification approval.
- No real anonymous posting until verification, moderation, admin, reporting, and safety controls exist.

Source basis:

- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: TSA Sensitive Security Information.
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: TSA Security Screening FAQ.
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: 49 U.S.C. 40119.
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: eHotelier - Airline crew security: how safe is your hotel?
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: FTC privacy/security sources.
- `RESEARCH_NOTES.md`: Blind FAQ and Blind Community Guidelines.

## 7. Wedge Assessment

Strongest wedge:

- Base Boards.
- Layover Boards.
- Verified anonymous Crew Rooms.

Weakest wedge:

- Nearby crew.
- Meetup.
- Social matching.

Most defensible moat:

- Community-generated aviation knowledge plus AI summaries.

Strategic interpretation:

- Deadhead Club should not pitch itself as a crew social app.
- It should pitch itself as an off-duty aviation-worker utility network built around verified knowledge, practical boards, and safe candid discussion.

## 8. Product Recommendations

Keep:

- Utility-first positioning.
- Base Boards.
- Layover Boards.
- Crew Rooms.
- Verified privately / anonymous publicly / accountable internally.
- Broader airline workforce inclusion.
- No-code waitlist validation.
- Public splash page as validation asset if explicitly approved.
- Private app shell only after explicit M1A approval.

Avoid in V1:

- Airline portal login.
- Schedule scraping/import.
- Non-rev load requests.
- Nearby crew radar.
- Dating/swiping.
- Exact crew hotel exposure.
- Public location tracking.
- Crash pad marketplace/payment.
- Badge uploads on public waitlist.
- AI final moderation or verification decisions.

## 9. Public Pitch Tightening

Avoid sounding like CrewVIP, Rendezvous, or Air Crew Meet. Do not lead with "connect with crew nearby."

Preferred direction:

> Real off-duty airline intel from verified airline people.

Supporting line:

> Base intel, layover knowledge, and honest crew talk - verified, private, and built for airline life.

Guardrails:

- Do not imply official airline affiliation.
- Do not imply legal/trademark clearance.
- Do not promise schedule import, flight-load requests, nearby crew tracking, dating, exact hotel exposure, or public launch.
